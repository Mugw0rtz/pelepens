About us

Welcome to our vibrant community! There’s no need for formal introductions here; we’re a group of passionate individuals united by a shared vision (and a shared love for snacks!). Our journey began with a simple idea: to collaborate, innovate, and support each other in our endeavors—while also making sure no one is left with the last slice of pizza.

Meet the crew: we are Andrea Ambrona, Shyla Marie Basa, Renalyn Cueva, Edmar Gonzales, Edson Sayon, and Michelle Trinidad. Each of us brings something special to the table—whether it’s Andrea’s knack for organizing chaos, Shyla’s ability to make even the most boring meetings fun, Renalyn’s incredible problem-solving skills (and her famous chocolate chip cookies), Edmar’s tech wizardry, Edson’s creativity that knows no bounds, or Michelle’s talent for keeping us all on track (even when we’d rather be brainstorming memes).

About us

Our story is one of collaboration. We started out as a small group, brainstorming over coffee (and a few too many donuts), sharing ideas and dreaming big. We quickly learned that the best ideas often come when you’re just trying to figure out what to order for lunch. Remember that time we tried to decide on a project theme and ended up planning a group outing instead? Yep, that’s us!

As we’ve grown, so have our ambitions. Together, we’ve tackled exciting projects, celebrated our successes, and turned setbacks into stepping stones (and occasionally, into hilarious inside jokes). Our group thrives on open communication, collaboration, and a genuine love for what we do. We believe in the power of teamwork—where every voice matters, every contribution counts, and every snack is fair game.

As we continue this journey, we invite you to join us! Let’s explore new horizons, spark innovative ideas, and create something truly amazing together. And don’t worry; we promise to save you some pizza. Welcome aboard!

The Epic Tale of the Paper Prototype Website

Once upon a time, in the bustling realm of Brainstormopolis, a band of six fearless creators embarked on a noble quest to design the ultimate website. Armed with nothing but their collective wit, a stack of printer paper, and an arsenal of colored markers, they ventured into the vast unknown—a world where ideas ruled and prototypes were king.

The leader, Andrea the Visionary, declared, “We shall forge this prototype with our bare hands and conquer the lands of user engagement!” Her words echoed through the room, stirring the spirits of the team.

Shyla, Keeper of the Ruler, stepped forward. “Let us draw precise rectangles, for without them, our website shall crumble into chaos!” She wielded her ruler like a sword, carving out perfect shapes on the sacred parchment.

Renalyn, the Mistress of Fonts, said, “Behold, the majesty of Comic Sans!” But her comrades gasped in horror. “No!” cried Edson the Minimalist. “Only Arial or Times New Roman can carry the weight of our vision!” A fierce debate ensued, lasting hours, until a compromise was reached: Verdana.

Edmar the Storyteller began sketching dramatic banners and buttons, narrating their functions with flair. “This button,” he proclaimed, “shall summon the mighty ‘Contact Us’ page. And here, a carousel of images so captivating, even Zeus would stop scrolling!”

Meanwhile, Michelle the Artisan unleashed her mastery of color theory. “Blue for trust! Green for growth! And red… for power!” With bold strokes, she breathed life into the prototype, turning it into a dazzling mosaic of hues.

As the day wore on, tensions rose. The Great Debate of Navigation Bars nearly tore the group apart. Should it be at the top? The side? Sticky or scrollable? Finally, unity was restored when Andrea devised the revolutionary “Floating Nav Bar of Destiny”—a feature so advanced it barely fit on the paper.

Hours turned into days, and their table became a battlefield of crumpled drafts and spilled coffee. Yet, through grit and determination, the final masterpiece emerged: a paper prototype so detailed, it seemed to hum with digital energy. Users (imaginary ones, of course) could almost click the hand-drawn buttons and scroll through the pretend pages.

Triumphantly, they presented their creation to the Council of Professors, who marveled at the craftsmanship. One elder remarked, “This… this is no ordinary prototype. It’s art.” And with those words, their victory was assured.

The six heroes returned to their studies, legends in their own right, their story told across the land. To this day, it is said that their paper prototype still resides in the halls of innovation, inspiring future creators to dream big and draw bigger.
